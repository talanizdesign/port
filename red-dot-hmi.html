Red Dot Best of the Best

A next-generation Human-Machine Interface for IMS Technologies, awarded Red Dot Best of the Best 2024 in the Design Concept category.

01 — Context

IMS Technologies, a global leader in industrial automation, aimed to reimagine its Human-Machine Interface (HMI) for a new generation of highly complex, data-driven machines. The challenge was to design an HMI that could simplify machine control while reflecting the company's forward-thinking product strategy and customer-centric approach.

02 — The Challenge

How could we deliver a next-generation HMI that enhances usability, reduces operator error, and elevates the product experience?

03 — Results
  • Awarded Red Dot "Best of the Best" 2024 in the Design Concept category, recognizing innovation and clarity in complex industrial systems.
  • Delivered a fully redesigned HMI that simplified user workflows and enhanced safety, usability, and machine interaction.
  • Strengthened the client's brand positioning as a leader in innovation and usability within industrial automation.
Watch: Red Dot Award Best of the Best 2024 — ►
04 — My Role

As Design Director, I led the multidisciplinary team responsible for delivering the full HMI experience, from research to design ready for dev. I oversaw international user research efforts, including on-site studies with clients in China, to uncover real-world needs in operational contexts.

I guided the definition of product features, concept development, wireframing, and final UI design — ensuring alignment between user insight, technical feasibility, and strategic goals.

05 — The Approach I Applied
  1. 01

    Research

    Comprehensive research covering market trends, user needs, and internal business goals. On-site fieldwork with clients, stakeholder interviews, and contextual inquiry to align the HMI with operational workflows and strategic positioning.

  2. 02

    Product Concept

    Translated insights into a clear product vision, defining key features and visual concept directions; synthesized findings into a cohesive value proposition balancing usability, brand, and technical feasibility.

  3. 03

    Product Design

    Iterative co-design sessions with the client developed wireframes, interaction flows, and pixel-perfect screens; assembled the right expertise, applied appropriate methodologies, and evolved internal leadership capabilities.
