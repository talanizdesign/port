Design Ops

Aligning multiple design teams — internal and external — around a shared operating model, so products evolve independently but feel like one family.

01 — Context

A multinational organization developing multiple digital products was working with several design teams, internal and external, operating independently. Over time, this resulted in fragmented user experiences, inconsistent design decisions, and products that felt unrelated despite belonging to the same ecosystem.

Each team optimized locally, but there was no shared way of working, no common design language, and no clear ownership across products — scaling design became increasingly difficult, and the product portfolio lacked coherence.

02 — The Challenge

How do you align multiple design teams around a shared product vision and operating model — so products evolve independently but still feel like part of the same system?

03 — Results
  • Unified multiple design teams under a shared Design Operations framework
  • Established consistent ways of working across teams while preserving local autonomy
  • Enabled products to present a coherent, family-like experience across a complex product ecosystem
  • Improved alignment between design, product, and engineering, reducing friction in delivery
  • Strengthened internal ownership and reduced reliance on fragmented external design support
  • Created a scalable foundation that continues to support growing design maturity over multiple years (now in its fourth year)
04 — My Role

As Design Director, led the definition and rollout of Design Operations across teams and products. Worked closely with design leadership, product owners, engineers, and external partners to align on operating principles, responsibilities, and delivery practices — focused on creating structures that let teams collaborate effectively at scale, beyond individual projects or artifacts.

05 — The Approach I Applied
  • Assessed how teams were working independently and identified points of fragmentation across products and disciplines
  • Defined a shared Design Operations framework covering roles, responsibilities, decision-making, and collaboration models
  • Established common workflows and rituals to align design, product, and engineering without introducing unnecessary bureaucracy
  • Introduced shared principles and standards to support coherence across products, while allowing teams to adapt locally
  • Embedded these practices into day-to-day delivery to ensure adoption and long-term sustainability

Note: a design system was developed as part of this work, serving as an operational enabler supporting consistency and efficiency within the broader Design Operations framework.
