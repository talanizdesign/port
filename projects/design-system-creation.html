Creation of a Design System

Four years, nine products, one enterprise design system built from zero — and eventually sold as a commercial service.

01 — Context

A global manufacturing enterprise (€12B revenue, 9 products across HMI, Web & Mobile) had design execution split across 4 external vendors with no shared standards — creating technical debt, zero component reusability, and a fragmented brand experience across products.

02 — The Challenge

Design a system that makes designing products repeatable, consistent, and independent of any individual contributor — without an existing single source of truth to build from.

03 — Results
2023 Creation

Design Ops and full Design System built and published; contract renewed, delivered within budget.

2024 Adoption

Design bankruptcy eliminated; system adopted across 5 products; DS capability trained into the internal team; contract renewed.

2025 Evolution

Product family unified under one system with variable-based theming; DS sold as a new commercial service to a second client (a company unifying 3 acquired brands); contract renewed.

2026 Expansion

DS positioned as a standalone commercial package; AI + design-system expertise combined into a new service offering.

04 — My Role

Design System Lead — built the internal business case and led client delivery across all 4 years; coached two Design Managers into fully autonomous ownership of the system; evolved from hands-on creation to strategic direction as the system matured into a saleable service.

05 — The Approach I Applied

A three-phase model repeated and matured each year: Research (stakeholder interviews, cross-product audits, single source of truth) → Product Definition (co-design sessions, governance model) → Product Design (assets + full documentation, AI-accelerated from 2023 onward).

Key inflection points: resolving a hidden colour-semantic conflict discovered mid-research, introducing a metrics layer after realizing adoption had no data behind it, and recognizing the system itself as a repeatable, sellable product.
