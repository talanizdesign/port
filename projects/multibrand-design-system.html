Multibrand Design System

A scalable foundation for a group integrating newly acquired brands — shared standards without forcing brand homogenization.

01 — Context

Part of a multi-year initiative to establish a scalable design system for a group undergoing rapid growth through acquisitions. As the company integrated newly acquired brands, it faced increasing fragmentation across digital interfaces, interaction patterns, and visual foundations.

Brands needed to retain individual identity while the organization needed a shared system for consistency, efficiency, and future scalability.

02 — The Challenge

Support post-acquisition integration without forcing brand homogenization — integrate newly acquired brands into a shared design system, preserve brand differentiation while introducing common foundations, support multiple machine interfaces and interaction modes, reduce fragmentation from parallel legacy systems, and enable future acquisitions to be onboarded without redesigning the system.

03 — Results
  • A structured multi-brand foundation supporting inherited and future brands
  • A unified core component library reusable across products and brands
  • Semantic variables enabling consistency without visual lock-in
  • Support for light and dark modes across all system layers
  • A scalable framework for onboarding newly acquired brands
  • Faster alignment between teams working on different products
  • A stable foundation for long-term interface evolution
04 — My Role

Led the project from a design leadership and delivery perspective, translating post-acquisition complexity into a clear, executable system roadmap. Led the design team across foundations, components, and variables; defined the roadmap with client stakeholders; designed the variables implementation strategy enabling scalable brand integration without restructuring; established a shared vision for progressive onboarding of newly acquired brands.

The variables roadmap was a strategic mechanism — not just managing visual differences between brands, but enabling controlled integration, consistency, and future acquisitions through a shared semantic layer.

05 — The Approach I Applied

Approached the work as a platform for integration, not just a design system:

  1. 01

    Acquisition-aware system design

    Structured to absorb new brands without structural refactoring.

  2. 02

    Shared foundations, differentiated expression

    Common variables and components enabled consistency while allowing brand-level customization.

  3. 03

    Incremental integration

    Newly acquired brands aligned progressively, without disrupting ongoing delivery.

  4. 04

    Semantic abstraction

    Semantic variables allowed legacy design decisions to be normalized over time.

  5. 05

    Operational sustainability

    Decisions accounted for governance, maintenance, and long-term adoption across a growing group.
